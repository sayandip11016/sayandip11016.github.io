My research explores the intriguing intersections between theoretical physics, quantum information, and condensed matter systems. Below are the primary areas of my active research and investigation.

## Quantum Many-Body Physics

In quantum many-body systems, I study the complex dynamics and entanglement structures that emerge from large sets of interacting particles. My focus is on understanding non-equilibrium dynamics and topological preservation in quantum matter.

-   **Dual-Unitary and Floquet Dynamics:** Exploring dual-unitary quantum circuits and Floquet transverse field Ising models coupled with duality twists to better understand operator spreading and entanglement growth.
-   **Quantum Coherence Transitions:** Characterizing the robustness of quantum coherence and examining the profound differences between ergodic dynamic phases and Many-Body Localized (MBL) systems under various topologies.
-   **Defect Dynamics:** Investigating the coarsening dynamics of non-solvable non-Abelian topological defects inside specialized geometries, such as non-equilibrium nematic liquid crystals.

## Quantum Network and Communication

Scaling the operational reach of quantum communication protocols relies on developing reliable hardware interfaces and exploring novel routing strategies capable of supporting global macroscopic quantum networks.

-   **Terahertz Detection Structures:** Theoretically modeling highly efficient Josephson junctions integrated with topological materials (BSCCO) and graphene architectures for advanced Terahertz radiation detection and sophisticated quantum sensing setups.
-   **Global Routing Strategies:** Formulating theoretical architectures for satellite-relayed global quantum communication systems capable of maintaining entanglement and high-fidelity operation without requiring intermediate quantum memory constraints.
-   **Topological Resilience:** Researching feasible implementation blueprints and network graphs for establishing protected logical Majorana zero modes inside realistic topological nanowire networks.

## Quantum Error Correction

Achieving fault-tolerant scalability is the central bottleneck to practical continuous-variable and qubit-based quantum computing systems. My work models and characterizes optimized encoding formats capable of mitigating common hardware-level issues.

-   **LDPC Implementations:** Developing and validating ultra-enhanced decoding frameworks customized for Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes specifically designed to remain strictly resilient under heavily biased operational noise environments.
-   **Topological Encodings:** Performing studies on Clifford-deformed tile codes to verify layout commutativity and systemic integrity across varying logical gate execution schedules.
-   **Numerical Evaluation:** Leveraging advanced simulation frameworks—including customized Tensor Networks algorithms, algorithmic exact diagonalization logic, and finite-bias Monte Carlo modeling packages—to trace operator error propagation pathways under structured error parameters.
